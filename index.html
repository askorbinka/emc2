About the app

E=M*C^2 is an app which allow you to plan your time, produce experiments to increase your efficiency and to track your results, find best working strategies and log them as your personal data.

The work of the app starts when you create a plan: choose its type - for now (any period of the day or night, just a quick checklist), for a day (daily planning), week, month and other periods of time, what lets you to create not only tactical but also strategic planning.

Next thing - after creating the plan, you can work with the app in two modes: be online and use special tools for time count and track your work to do and done (you should press the button "done"). While you make a plan, you can write an estimate time, which the app will use to count how many things you can do in definite period of time, so you could write a number of reserved hours for different tasks and rely on the app. It will tell you if your estimate was exaggerated and you have no time for other things + it will suggest you to prioretize your tasks to do the most important things. And all that just on the fly, you won't give it more than 5 minutes drinking tea.

The second mode is much simpler - you just create plan and it works just like a checklist - no reminds, no time counter etc., but you can still log your time.

There is a special department in the data grid for urgent unplanned tasks, which will be count too.

And the process of counting your productivity is this: the app looks at the difference between your estimation and logged time (or the tasks finalized and left unfinished + your logged time) and forms the stats for you, including:

  • difference in time = error coefficient
  • difference in planed volume of work and fact volume = max amount of tasks per different periods according to their difficuty (5-grade scale)
  • difference in amount of planned and unplanned tasks = time loss coefficient and con
  • tendency of your time spending
  • tendency of your productivity
  • and some other...
The app will not only generate the stats for you, but also let you to make some analysis of it: evaluate your productivity, time, errors, all that stuff. These data won't be used for computing, it's just for you to take seriously your time and life.

After the system generates yout stats, you can enable the auto-tips: when creating a new plan the app will limit the amount of tasks and time accroding to the general stats tend. So it will suggest you the best working strategy.
